### SECTION 3 — NOVELTY BREAKDOWN
- **Is your FULL system already done somewhere?**  
  No **single** published system with *exactly* your stack (YOLOv8 + custom contamination CNN + per‑item valuation + visual dashboard) stands out in the results. However, **all the pieces are present in the literature**, often in slightly different arrangements. Your pipeline is therefore **new in layout, not in kind**.

- **Are individual components already known?**  
  - **Module 1 (YOLOv8 waste detection):** Common (e.g., two‑stage YOLOv8 work, many CNN waste classifiers).  
  - **Module 2 (contamination CNN):** Very common; liquid‑contamination and general “how dirty is this item” CNNs are explicitly studied.  
  - **Module 3 (valuation formulas):** The cash‑ and CO₂‑valuation formulas (`cash ≈ weight × price × purity`, `CO₂ saved ≈ weight × carbon‑factor × purity`) are standard in recycling‑ROI tools; academically, this is a **simple LCA‑style monetization**, not a novel metric.  
  - **Techniques (TL, YOLOv8, CNN, depthwise convs, softmax, regression, BN, dropout, augmentation):** All textbook / standard in 2023–2026 computer‑vision workflows.

- **Is your combination obvious or non‑trivial?**  
  - Obvious: “detect waste → compute contamination → monetize it” is a natural pipeline for waste management AI.  
  - Non‑trivial (but not novel *per se*): stitching real‑time YOLOv8 + per‑item cropped contamination CNN + lightweight valuation engine and a dashboard is an **engineering effort**; it’s complex and useful, but not a new research concept.

- **Does CRIR‑style metric already exist under another name?**  
  Your “pureness” factor inside valuation is a proxy for **material quality / contamination level**.  
  - In industry: this is often called **recyclate quality**, **contamination rate**, or **purity factor** in recycling‑ROI calculators and LCA‑style reports (e.g., “each ton of 90%‑pure plastic saves X kg CO₂”).  
  - In research: papers talk about **contamination‑corrected recyclability** or **quality‑adjusted recycling yield**, but not under a branded “CRIR”‑style acronym.  
  - So: **concept exists, named differently**; your “purity” is not a novel metric, just a re‑labeling of existing recycling‑quality ideas.
